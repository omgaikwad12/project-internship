Monstera Deliciosa

Light: Prefers bright, indirect sunlight. Too much direct sun can scorch the leaves.

Water: Water thoroughly when the top 2 inches of soil feel dry. Avoid letting it sit in water.

Soil: Use a peat-based, well-draining potting mix.

Toxicity: Mildly toxic to humans and toxic to cats and dogs if ingested.

Key Feature: The iconic splits in its leaves, called fenestrations, help it withstand strong winds in its native habitat.

×

Fiddle Leaf Fig

Light: Needs lots of bright, filtered light. A spot near an east-facing window is often perfect.

Water: Water when the top inch of soil is dry. They are sensitive to overwatering.

Soil: Requires a well-draining soil mix to prevent waterlogging.

Toxicity: Toxic to cats and dogs.

Key Feature: Known for being a bit dramatic and will drop its leaves if moved too often or exposed to drafts.

×

Snake Plant

Light: Extremely tolerant. Survives in low light but thrives in bright, indirect light.

Water: Drought-tolerant. Water only when the soil is completely dry. Overwatering is its biggest enemy.

Soil: Prefers a sandy, well-draining mix, like cactus soil.

Toxicity: Mildly toxic to pets and humans if ingested.

Key Feature: A top-rated air-purifying plant, known for converting CO2 into oxygen at night.

×

Golden Pothos

Light: Very adaptable. Does well in low light to bright, indirect light. Variegation is more pronounced with more light.

Water: Water when the top inch of soil feels dry. It's quite forgiving if you forget.

Soil: Any standard, well-draining potting soil will do.

Toxicity: Toxic to cats and dogs.

Key Feature: Often called "devil's ivy" because it's almost impossible to kill and stays green even in the dark.

×

Pilea Peperomioides

Light: Prefers bright, indirect light. Direct sun will burn the leaves.

Water: Water when the top 1-2 inches of soil are dry. The leaves will droop when thirsty.

Soil: Use a well-aerated, fast-draining potting mix.

Toxicity: Non-toxic and safe for pets and humans.

Key Feature: Nicknamed the "friendship plant" because it produces many baby plantlets that are easy to share.

×

Calathea

Light: Prefers medium to bright indirect light. Direct sun will fade the leaf patterns.

Water: Keep soil consistently moist. Use filtered or distilled water if possible.

Soil: Needs a moisture-retentive but well-draining mix.

Toxicity: Non-toxic and pet-safe.

Key Feature: Leaves fold up at night, like hands in prayer, and lower during the day.

×

Aloe Vera

Light: Loves bright, direct sunlight.

Water: Needs very little water. Water deeply but infrequently, allowing soil to dry out completely.

Soil: A cactus or succulent mix with excellent drainage is essential.

Toxicity: Gel is medicinal, but the outer skin is toxic to pets.

Key Feature: The soothing gel inside its leaves has been used for centuries to treat burns and skin irritations.

×

Money Tree

Light: Thrives in bright, indirect light but can adapt to lower light.

Water: Water thoroughly when the top 2-4 inches of soil are dry.

Soil: A well-draining, nutrient-rich soil is best.

Toxicity: Non-toxic and pet-safe.

Key Feature: Often sold with a braided trunk, it is a symbol of good luck and prosperity in Feng Shui.
